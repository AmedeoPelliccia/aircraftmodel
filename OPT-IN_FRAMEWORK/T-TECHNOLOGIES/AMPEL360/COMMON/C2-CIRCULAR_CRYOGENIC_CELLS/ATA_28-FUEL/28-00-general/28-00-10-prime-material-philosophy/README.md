## Premise

In the BWB hydrogen-hybrid architecture, **H₂ is treated as a prime material,
not as a fuel.** This reframing is normative across the C² CELL paradigm:

- A *fuel* is consumed in an open combustion cycle whose only product is heat
  (and exhaust). Its hazards are dominated by **flame**.
- A *prime material* (PM) is a chemically stored potential cycled through a
  reversible, electrochemical loop (storage ⇄ conditioning ⇄ conversion ⇄
  regeneration). Its hazards are dominated by **phase instability**.

## Implications for ATA-28

1. The chapter title *FUEL* is retained for ATA-100 / iSpec 2200 / BREX
   compliance only. Internally the system is governed as a **PM custody loop.**
2. Sub-chapter labels (`28-10` Storage, `28-20` Distribution, `28-30` Dump,
   `28-40` Indicating) keep their canonical ATA semantics; the PM-specific
   technology lives in named subsections under each.
3. Reserved slots `28-50`, `28-60`, `28-70` host genuinely new functions
   (H₂ leak detection, cryogenic thermal management, embrittlement monitoring)
   that have no analogue in the legacy fuel paradigm. Each must be declared
   in the project BREX with an extension justification.
